Geometry-aware two-point vector and split-form divergence for 3-D.

The MappedDivergence routine follows the construction described in

Winters, Kopriva, Gassner, Hindenlang, "Construction of Modern Robust Nodal Discontinuous Galerkin Spectral Element Methods for the Compressible Navier-Stokes Equations", Lecture Notes in Computational Science and Engineering, 2021.

The contravariant two-point flux in the r-th computational direction is

F~^r_{(i,n),j,k} = sum_d (Ja^r_d(i,j,k) + Ja^r_d(n,j,k))/2 * f^d_{(i,n),j,k}

and similarly for the xi^2 and xi^3 sums (averaging (i,j,k)-(i,n,k) and (i,j,k)-(i,j,n) respectively). The physical divergence is

(1/J_{i,j,k}) * 2 * sum_n [ D_{n,i} F~^1 + D_{n,j} F~^2 + D_{n,k} F~^3 ]

interior(n,i,j,k,iEl,iVar,d) stores the physical-space two-point flux f^d in the d-th physical direction.
